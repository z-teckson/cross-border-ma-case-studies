# Daimler-Benz / Chrysler Merger Case Brief

## Basic Facts
- **Announcement Date**: May 7, 1998
- **Completion Date**: November 12, 1998
- **Deal Structure**: Stock‑for‑stock merger billed as a "merger of equals"
- **Initial Valuation**: ~$38 billion (combined market capitalization)
- **Key Players**:
  - Daimler-Benz AG: Jürgen Schrempp (CEO), Hilmar Kopper (Chairman of Supervisory Board)
  - Chrysler Corporation: Robert Eaton (Co‑Chairman), Thomas Stallkamp (President)

## Deal Rationale
- Geographic diversification: Daimler (Europe) + Chrysler (North America)
- Product‑line complementarity: luxury sedans & trucks (Daimler) + mass‑market cars & minivans (Chrysler)
- Expected synergies: $1.4 billion annually from purchasing, R&D, and platform sharing

## Governance Structure Post‑Merger
- Dual headquarters: Stuttgart (Germany) and Auburn Hills, Michigan (USA)
- Co‑Chairmen: Jürgen Schrempp and Robert Eaton
- Board composition: German two‑tier model adapted with an "Integrative Board" (Aufsichtsrat) and a "Management Board" (Vorstand) that included former Chrysler executives.

## Subsequent Timeline Highlights
- 1999‑2000: First signs of cultural clashes and integration difficulties
- 2001: Schrempp assumes sole CEO role; Eaton retires
- 2003‑2005: Repeated profit warnings, Chrysler division losses
- 2007: DaimlerChrysler AG sells 80.1% of Chrysler to Cerberus Capital Management, effectively ending the merger.
